Key Findings & Recommendations

The 8 Things That Matter Most

Actionable insights from the quarter's data

01

Landing Page Builder Is a Quality Crisis

73 bugs, 0 PBIs, consuming 2 developers (Marcin, Tymoteusz). This area is generating more defects than any other initiative while delivering zero new features.

Recommend: Pause new features. Execute a quality stabilization sprint. Add automated test coverage before resuming feature work.
02

Scope Creep Is Undermining Sprint Predictability

28.7% average mid-sprint additions. Sprint planning becomes less meaningful when nearly a third of work materializes after commitment.

Recommend: Enforce sprint commitment freeze after day 1. Route emergent work to a 10–15% "interrupt budget" reserved capacity.
03

Bug Ratio Is Trending Up

Bug-to-feature ratio hit 66.7% in Sprint 133 — two-thirds of deliverable items were bugs, not features. The team is spending more time fixing than building.

Recommend: Invest in shift-left testing. Add acceptance criteria reviews before sprint commitment. Consider quality gates.
04

Key-Person Dependency on Thomas Holland

One person owns the entire EventFlow platform (93+ items across Core, Segmentation, Batch Jobs, Change Feed). This is a bus-factor risk for the organization.

Recommend: Establish a cross-training plan. Begin pair programming rotations with a second EventFlow contributor.
05

GitHub Enterprise Migration Was a Team Win

98% completion, 6 contributors, 50 items across 5 sprints. This is the model for how to execute a cross-team initiative: clear ownership, shared accountability, phased delivery.

Apply this playbook to future cross-cutting initiatives (e.g., .NET 10 upgrade).
06

Notification System Needs a Dedicated Push

6 sprints at 46.7% done. It has been simmering without focus — no sprint had it as a primary objective. At the current pace, it will take another full quarter.

Recommend: Make it a primary initiative for 2 sprints with a dedicated owner and clear exit criteria.
07

Sprint 134 Shows Overcommitment Patterns

327 items with several team members below 10% completion mid-sprint: Justin Barreira (5.9%), Adam Wojciechowski (7.1%). The sprint was loaded beyond capacity.

Recommend: Review capacity planning assumptions. Use trailing 3-sprint velocity as commitment ceiling.
08

Estimation Accuracy Was Improving

Hour burn rate improved from 58% to 84% over S129–S133, showing growing estimation maturity. S134 at 35.4% may reflect in-flight work or over-commitment rather than regression.

Continue tracking burn rate as a health metric. Investigate S134 burn at sprint close to determine if trend holds.
